01 / THE PROBLEM

Flashcards encourage passive learning through contextless memorization. This reinforces L1 interference by relying on translations instead of active usage.

02 / THE ACTION

I developed a local web app (Flash Denken) based on a better way to learn vocabulary. This learning platform provides contextualized and active learning/recall experience.

03 / THE RESULT

My app was a big part of how I reached a B2 level in Dutch only a year after moving here.

Inspiration & Context

I moved to the Netherlands in Fall 2024 and was motivated to learn Dutch from the first day. I knew that I could do it myself without any course or specific books. I began thinking about how can I design and pursue the learning experience while immersing myself in the language.

One of the first things I did was to create a local flash card app that was basically a digital way to have physical traditional flash cards.

After a while, I was not satisfied with the progress. Specifically, I could recognize the words in text but I could not actively use them when needed or understand them when they were spoken.

I kept thinking and questioning about the learning process. I started from the first principles:

What is language?

One limited way to think about human language is as a tool that we use to communicate intent.

How do we use language?

This is about context. We almost never use or encounter a word in isolation. We use them embeded in a context where we react or comment on something, for instance. The simplified way I mentally modelled language usage is as follows, lets say for learning the phrase "Turn down the volume":

  • Situation: "I haven't slept well last night. The neighbours are too noisy."
  • Inner Dialogue: "I have to let them know that I need them to be quieter."
  • Expression: "Hello, could you please turn down the volume?"
What is the process of storing and retrieving words in the brain?

The word storage here actually is problematic. We don't store words like a static list in our brain.
